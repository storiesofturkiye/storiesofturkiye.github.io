Once upon a time, there was a curious little cat named Karamel, who loved stories and adventures. Karamel set off on a journey across Turkey to discover the magical tales and legends of each region.

Marama Region - Istanbul: The Legend of the Maiden’s Tower Karamel’s first stop was Istanbul, where she learned about the legend of the Maiden's Tower. The tower stood tall on a small islet in the Bosphorus, holding many secrets and tales.

Aegean Region – Izmir: The Story of Ephesus Next, Karamel traveled to Izmir to hear the story of Ephesus. The ancient ruins spoke of a grand city with temples, theaters, and bustling markets.

Mediterranean Region - Antalya: The Tale of the Chimera In Antalya, Karamel heard the tale of the Chimera, a mythical fire-breathing creature. She visited the site where flames still burn from the rocks, a testament to the legend.

Central Anatolia - Cappadocia: The Fairy Chimneys and Cave Dwellings In Central Anatolia, Karamel explored Cappadocia and learned about the fairy chimneys and the people who once lived in the cave dwellings, hidden from invaders.

Black Sea Region - Trabzon: The Legend of Sumela Monastery Karamel then traveled to Trabzon in the Black Sea region to hear the legend of Sumela Monastery. Built high on a cliff, the monastery was a place of mystery and reverence.

Eastern Anatolia – Mount Ağrı: The Legend of Noah’s Ark In Eastern Anatolia, Karamel visited Mount Ağrı, the legendary resting place of Noah’s Ark. The snow-capped mountain was a place of wonder and ancient tales.

Southeast Anatolia – Gobekli Tepe: The World's Oldest Temple Karamel’s final stop was Gobekli Tepe in Southeastern Anatolia, where she explored the ancient site believed to be the world’s oldest temple and learned about the mysteries it held.
