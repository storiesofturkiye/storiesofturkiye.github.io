Southeastern Anatolia - Göbekli Tepe: Ancient Ruins and Wildlife.
Karamel’s last stop was Göbekli Tepe. There, she found amazing old ruins! She also watched the animals that lived in that place. .
Göbekli Tepe was a very special place from a long, long time ago. People who lived there built giant stone circles. These circles had pictures of animals like foxes, lions, and birds. No one knows exactly why they built them, but they were very important to the people of Göbekli Tepe..
Karamel looked closely at the stones. Some of them were very big! She saw birds carved into the stone. They looked like they were flying. There were also animals with big teeth, like lions and foxes. It was like looking at a story from the past. .
While she was there, Karamel saw all sorts of wildlife. She saw deer walking in the grass. Birds were singing in the trees. Sometimes, she even saw wild dogs running around! The animals seemed to be very comfortable living near the ancient ruins. .
Göbekli Tepe showed Karamel how people long ago lived and what they believed in. It was a wonderful place to learn about history and see the animals that share this special land.
