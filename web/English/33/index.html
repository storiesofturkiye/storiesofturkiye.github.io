Once upon a time, there was a curious little cat named Karamel who loved music and dancing. One sunny day, Karamel found a magical tambourine that could transport her to different parts of Türkiye. Excited by the discovery, Karamel decided to embark on a musical journey to explore the rich musical traditions and dances of Türkiye.
4
5
Marmara – Roman Music in Istanbul Karamel’s first stop was Istanbul, where she was greeted by the lively sounds of Roman music. She watched in awe as the musicians played their vibrant tunes, and she couldn't resist dancing to the rhythm.

7
Aegean – A Zeybek dance in İzmir Next, Karamel traveled to İzmir, where she witnessed the powerful and elegant Zeybek dance. The dancers moved with such grace and strength that Karamel felt inspired to try the moves herself.

9
Mediterranean – Yörük Music in Antalya In Antalya, Karamel experienced the vibrant tunes of Yörük music. The lively melodies and colorful dances of the Yörük people made Karamel’s heart race with joy.

Central Anatolia – Whirling Dervishes in Konya Karamel then visited Konya, where she watched the mystical Whirling Dervishes perform their spiritual dance. The graceful spinning of the dervishes left Karamel mesmerized and filled her with peace.

13
Black Sea – Horon dance in Trabzon In Trabzon, Karamel joined in the energetic Black Sea region making his paws move with excitement.

Eastern Anatolia - Kemençe music in Erzurum Karamel’s journey took her to Erzurum, where she listened to the haunting melodies of Kemençe. The soulful music touched Karamel's heart and made her appreciate the deep cultural roots of the region.

17
Southeast Anatolia - Halay Dance in Diyarbakır Finally, Karamel arrived in Diyarbakır, where she joined in the rhythmic Halay dance. The unity and joy of the dancers made Karamel feel like she was part of a big, happy family.

With her heart full of melodies and her paws tired from dancing, Karamel realized that Turkey’s true beauty lay in its rich musical traditions and dances. She returned home, dreaming of the wonderful music and dances she had experienced.
