The Caves of Cappadocia Karamel's first stop was in Cappadocia, where she explored the fairy chimneys and ancient cave dwellings. She marveled at the unique rock formations and the history carved into the stone.

The Karain Cave Karamel then traveled to the Karain Cave, one of the oldest inhabited caves in Türkiye. She discovered ancient tools and artifacts left by early humans, learning about the cave's historical significance.

The Dim Cave In Alanya, Karamel explored the Dim Cave. She was fascinated by the stalactites and stalagmites that decorated the cave. The cool and mysterious atmosphere made her feel like she was in another world.

The Dupnisa Cave In Kırklareli, Karamel visited the Dupnisa Cave. She learned about the cave's two sections: the wet and dry caves. Exploring both, she discovered unique rock formations and bat colonies.

The Yedi Göller Cave Karamel's final stop was the Yedi Göller Cave in Kastamonu. She was captivated by the seven small lakes inside the cave, each with its own unique charm. The serenity of the cave made it a perfect end to her journey.
