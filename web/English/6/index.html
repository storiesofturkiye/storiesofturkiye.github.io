Once upon a time, there was a curious little cat named Karamel who loved to discover new things. Karamel set off on a journey across Türkiye to explore the beautiful and intricate world of Turkish handicrafts.
4
5
Marmara Region - Iznik Tiles Karamel's first stop was in Iznik, where she discovered the beautiful Iznik tiles. These tiles, with their bright colors and intricate patterns, have been used to decorate mosques and palaces for centuries.
6
7
Aegean Region - Ephesus Rugs Next, Karamel traveled to Ephesus, where she saw artisans weaving beautiful rugs. These rugs, made from wool and natural dyes, told stories of the region's rich history and culture.
8
9
Mediterranean Region - Antalya: Copper Work In Antalya, Karamel discovered the art of copper work. Artisans skillfully hammered and shaped copper into beautiful utensils and decorative items.
10
11
Central Anatolia - Cappadocia: Pottery In Cappadocia, Karamel visited a pottery workshop. She saw how the soft clay from the region was shaped into beautiful pots and vases, then fired in kilns to make them durable.
12
13
Black Sea Region - Trabzon: Silver Filigree Karamel then traveled to Trabzon, where she learned about the delicate art of silver filigree. Artisans twisted and braided fine silver wires into intricate designs, creating beautiful jewelry.
14
15
Eastern Anatolia - Van: Kilim Weaving In Van, Karamel discovered the art of kilim weaving. These flat-woven rugs, made from wool, featured bold geometric patterns and vibrant colors.
16
17
Southeastern Anatolia - Gaziantep: Copper Coffee Pots Karamel's final stop was Gaziantep, where she saw artisans making traditional copper coffee pots. These beautifully decorated pots were used to brew Turkish coffee.

19
With her heart full of admiration for the artisans and their beautiful creations, Karamel realized that Türkiye's true treasures were its rich traditions and the people who kept them alive. She returned home, dreaming of her next adventure.
