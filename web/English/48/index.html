Karamel, the curious little cat, wanted to explore the evolution of Turkish art. She decided to travel through time to see how Turkish art has changed, from long ago to today. With excitement in her heart, she set off on a new adventure.
4
5
Ancient Anatolian Art.. Çatalhöyük Karamel’s first stop was Çatalhöyük, one of the oldest places in the whole world where people lived! She looked with wonder at the very old pictures painted on the walls and the things people left behind. These pictures and things showed how the first people who lived there spent their days.
6
7
Hittite Art - Boğazköy. Next, Karamel went to Boğazköy. Boğazköy was where the ancient Hittite people lived and ruled, and it was their capital city called Hattusa. She looked at the amazing pictures carved into the rocks and the very large buildings.
8
9
Roman and Byzantine Art - Ephesus. In Ephesus, Karamel discovered the amazing world of Roman and Byzantine art. She walked through the old city, looking at beautiful mosaics and shiny marble statues. . The mosaics were like pictures made of tiny colored tiles. They told stories of brave heroes and happy times. The marble statues were so lifelike, they looked like real people! Karamel loved seeing all the beautiful art from so long ago. It showed how clever and creative the Roman and Byzantine people were. She spent the whole day exploring and admiring everything she saw. It was a wonderful adventure!
10
11
Seljuk Art - Konya Karamel then visited Konya, a city with lots of beautiful Seljuk art and buildings. She looked around at the mosques, which were decorated so nicely, and at the detailed tile work everywhere.
12
13
Ottoman Art - Topkapi Palace, Istanbul. In Istanbul, Karamel explored the Ottoman art and architecture at Topkapi Palace. She really liked the fancy decorations and the beautiful writing.
14
15
Modern Turkish Art - Istanbul Modern.. Karamel's next adventure took her to the Istanbul Modern Museum! There, she got to see paintings and sculptures made by modern Turkish artists. The art was so colorful and different – it really inspired her! She loved all the ways the artists showed their feelings and ideas.
16
17
Street Art - Kadıköy, Istanbul. Finally, Karamel explored the street art of Kadıköy, Istanbul. She walked through the streets, amazed by the creativity and messages conveyed through murals and graffiti.. She saw colorful pictures painted on the walls. Some were big and showed animals, like playful cats and friendly dogs. Others were smaller and had words written on them. . The street artists used spray paint to make these pictures. They created amazing designs with bright colors – reds, blues, yellows, and greens! Karamel looked closely at a mural with a smiling face. It made her feel happy.. She saw graffiti everywhere! Graffiti is like street art, but sometimes it's just someone's name or a short message. Some of the graffiti was funny, and some was thought-provoking. . Karamel loved seeing all the different kinds of art. Each picture and word told a story. She learned that street art can make people smile, think, and even feel inspired! Kadıköy was full of amazing surprises for her to discover.

Karamel came home with lots of new ideas in her heart and a lot of interesting things in her mind about how Turkish art has changed over time. She thought about how Turkish art is like a beautiful woven picture made with stories from the past and lots of creativity.
