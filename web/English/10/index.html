Once upon a time, there was a curious little cat named Karamel who loved exploring historical places. Karamel set off on a journey across Turkey to discover its magnificent historical and architectural landmarks.

Marmara Region - Istanbul: Hagia Sophia Karamel's first stop was in Istanbul, where she visited the magnificent Hagia Sophia. She marveled at its grand dome and beautiful mosaics, learning about its history as both a church and a mosque.

7
Aegean Region – Izmir: Ephesus Then, Karamel travelled to Izmir to visit the ancient city of Ephesus. She explored the grand Library of Celsus and the Great Theatre, imagining the bustling life of this ancient city.

Mediterranean Region - Antalya: Aspendos Theatre In Antalya, Karamel visited the ancient Aspendos Theatre. She marveled at the well-preserved structure and imagined the performances that once took place there.

11
Central Anatolia – Cappadocia: Göreme Open Air Museum In Cappadocia, Karamel explored the Göreme Open Air Museum. She wandered through the rock-cut churches and monasteries, amazed by the ancient frescoes.

13
Black Sea Region – Trabzon: Sumela Monastery Then Karamel traveled to Trabzon, where she visited the Sumela Monastery, perched high on a cliff. She marveled at the breathtaking views and the intricate details of the monastery.

Eastern Anatolia – Ani Ruins In Eastern Anatolia, Karamel visited the ancient city of Ani, known as the “City of 1001 Churches.” She explored the ruins and learned about the city’s historical significance.

Southeast Anatolia – Nemrut Mountain Karamel’s final destination was Nemrut Mountain, where she found the colossal statues and the ancient tomb of King Antiochus I. She was amazed by the scale of the site.

With her heart full of awe and her mind filled with history, Karamel realized that Türkiye’s true treasures were its magnificent landmarks and the stories they told. She returned home, dreaming of her next adventure.
