Karamel's Journey. Once upon a time, there was a curious little cat named Karamel who loved to discover new things. Karamel decided to go on an adventure all across Türkiye to explore the beautiful and detailed world of Turkish handicrafts. . Karamel walked through colorful towns and visited cozy workshops. She saw people making amazing things with their hands – beautiful carpets, delicate pottery, and sparkly jewelry. . She watched a weaver carefully create a carpet with colorful threads. A potter shaped clay into lovely bowls and plates. A jeweler added tiny stones to sparkling necklaces. Karamel was amazed by all the different ways people used their skills.. Karamel learned that each handcrafted item had a story. The colors, the patterns, and the shapes all told something about the person who made it and the place where it came from. . After a long journey, Karamel returned home with a heart full of wonderful memories and a great appreciation for the art of Turkish handicrafts. She knew that even the smallest things could be beautiful when made with love and skill.

Marmara Region - Iznik Tiles. Karamel’s first stop was in Iznik, and she found the most beautiful Iznik tiles! These tiles have bright colors and very detailed designs. People have used them to decorate mosques and palaces for many, many years.

Aegean Region - Ephesus Rugs.. Next, Karamel went to Ephesus. There, she saw people making beautiful rugs. They were weaving them! These rugs were made with wool and colors from nature. The colors were made from plants and other natural things. The rugs showed stories about the history of the area and how people lived there a long, long time ago.

Mediterranean Region - Antalya: Copper Work In Antalya. In Antalya, a place by the sea, a person named Karamel learned how to work with copper. Skilled artists carefully hit and shaped copper with tools. They made beautiful things like pots, bowls, and decorations. These copper items were not only useful but also very pretty!

Central Anatolia - Cappadocia: Pottery In Cappadocia. In Cappadocia, Karamel went to a pottery workshop. She watched how the soft clay from this area was made into lovely pots and vases. Then, they were baked in special ovens called kilns to make them strong and long-lasting.

Black Sea Region - Trabzon: Silver Filigree Karamel then traveled to Trabzon, where she learned about the delicate art of silver filigree. Artisans twisted and braided fine silver wires into intricate designs, creating beautiful jewelry. . They made tiny flowers, swirling patterns, and sparkling shapes. It took a lot of patience and skill to make each piece. The silver wires were so thin, like strands of hair! The artisans worked carefully, shaping the silver with tiny tools. They created necklaces, earrings, and bracelets that were truly works of art. Silver Filigree Karamel was amazed by how beautiful and detailed the jewelry was. She practiced twisting and braiding the silver, trying her best to create her own designs. It was hard work, but she loved learning this special skill in Trabzon.

Eastern Anatolia - Van: Kilim Weaving In Van. In Van, a place in Eastern Anatolia, a person named Karamel learned how to make kilim rugs. Kilim rugs are flat rugs made with wool. They have strong shapes and designs, like triangles and diamonds, and they are very colorful!

Southeastern Anatolia - Gaziantep: Copper Coffee Pots. Karamel’s last place to visit was Gaziantep. There, she watched people who were making special, old-fashioned copper coffee pots. These pots were decorated in beautiful ways. They were used for making Turkish coffee.
