Karamel, the adventurous little cat, was always fascinated by the hustle and bustle of markets. One day, she decided to explore the famous markets and bazaars of Türkiye. With a list of places to visit and excitement in her heart, she set off on a new adventure.
4
5
Grand Bazaar in Istanbul.. Karamel's first stop was the Grand Bazaar in Istanbul, one of the oldest and biggest covered markets in the whole world. She walked through the winding paths of shops, looking at all the colorful things. There were spices, shiny jewelry, and so many other treasures!
6
7
Spice Bazaar in Istanbul.. Next, Karamel visited the Spice Bazaar, which was also in Istanbul. The air smelled wonderful! It was filled with the strong smells of spices, plants, and different kinds of tea. She tried some yummy Turkish sweets and bought some spices to bring back home.
8
9
Kemeraltı Market in Izmir.. In Izmir, Karamel went to the Kemeraltı Market. It's a very old market that is always full of life! She loved how lively it was. She met kind people who were selling things and found lots of special handmade items.
10
11
Kemeraltı Food Market in Izmir went into the food part of the market. She tried yummy street food! There were fresh simit, which are like circular bread rings, and delicious kebabs. She learned all about how people in Izmir make their food, a special tradition.
12
13
Antalya Bazaar.. In Antalya, Karamel went to the local bazaar. She found beautiful carpets that were made by hand! She also saw traditional Turkish pottery. Karamel was very impressed by the detailed designs and how carefully the things were made.
14
15
Gaziantep Copper Bazaar Karamel's next stop was the Copper Bazaar in Gaziantep. She watched artists making beautiful things out of copper with skill. She learned about special ways of working with copper that have been passed down from families for a very long time.
16
17
Mardin Market Karamel's last stop was the market in Mardin, where she learned about the special history and traditions of the area. She looked at beautiful, detailed jewelry. She tried yummy food that was made locally. And she felt the kindness and warmth of the people who were selling things.
18
19
Karamel returned home with a happy heart and bags full of wonderful things. She understood that Turkish markets weren't just places to buy things. They were places to feel the amazing culture of Türkiye and how friendly the people are.
