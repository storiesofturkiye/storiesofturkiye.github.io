Göbekli Tepe and the Sumerians .The Göbekli Tepe site in Turkey has led archaeologists to reconsider the timeline of civilization, suggesting that complex religious structures may predate the known temple architecture of the Sumerians. The monumental enclosures at Göbekli Tepe, dating back to the 10th millennium BCE, feature intricately carved T-shaped pillars adorned with depictions of animals and abstract symbols. These pillars suggest a sophisticated understanding of engineering and ritual practice. The Sumerians, who emerged in Mesopotamia around the 4th millennium BCE, are traditionally credited with the invention of writing, agriculture, and urban centers. However, the discovery of Göbekli Tepe challenges this narrative by demonstrating the existence of complex religious communities and monumental architecture thousands of years earlier. The intricate carvings and astronomical alignments at Göbekli Tepe suggest that it served as a center for ritual and communal gatherings. Some researchers believe that the site may have influenced the development of temple architecture in Mesopotamia, including the ziggurats of Sumer. temples in the world.
