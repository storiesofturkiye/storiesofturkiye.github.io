Karamel the little cat was very curious. She loved to explore new places and learn about different things. One day, Karamel decided she wanted to learn about Türkiye! She wanted to find out what people in Türkiye wear – their special clothes and how they dress. . She started by visiting a library. The librarian showed her beautiful pictures of women wearing colorful headscarves and flowing dresses. There were men wearing vests and sometimes fezzes, which are like soft, round hats.. Karamel saw pictures of embroidered jackets with shiny beads and threads. Some clothes were made of soft cotton, perfect for the warm weather. Others were made of wool, to keep people warm in the cooler months. . She learned that different parts of Türkiye had their own special styles of clothing. The colors were bright and cheerful – red, blue, green, and yellow! Sometimes, they used patterns with flowers and stars.. Karamel was amazed by all the beautiful clothes. She thought it was wonderful how each piece of clothing told a story about the people who wore them and where they came from. She decided she wanted to learn even more about Türkiye's clothes!

Marmara Region - Istanbul: Ottoman Attire Karamel’s first stop was Istanbul. There, she saw the beautiful clothes of the Ottoman people. The clothes were made from rich fabrics, and they had very detailed and pretty designs. This amazed her a lot!

Aegean Region - Izmir: Zeybek Costumes. Next, Karamel traveled to Izmir. There, she saw the traditional Zeybek costumes! They were so beautiful. The vests were brightly colored, and the trousers were wide. They looked perfect for the lively Zeybek dance.

Mediterranean Region - Antalya: Yoruk Attire. In Antalya, Karamel found something very interesting! She discovered the traditional clothes of the Yoruks. The Yoruks are a group of people who used to travel around a lot. . Their clothes were made to be useful for traveling. They were also very pretty and had beautiful decorations on them.

Central Anatolia - Ankara: Seğmen Kostümleri Karamel then traveled to Ankara, where she saw the Seğmen costumes. The traditional clothing of the Seğmen dancers was elegant and impressive.

Black Sea Region - Trabzon: Kemençe Players' Attire. In Trabzon, Karamel saw how the people who play the kemençe dress. They wear loose, black trousers and bright, colorful shirts. This kind of clothing is just right for the fun and lively Horon dance!

Eastern Anatolia - Erzurum: Bar Costumes in Erzurum. In Erzurum, Karamel saw the beautiful costumes for the Bar dance! The dancers wore clothes with very detailed and colorful embroidery. These clothes showed how special and unique their culture is.

Southeastern Anatolia - Mardin: Traditional Dresses. Karamel’s last stop was Mardin. She really liked the traditional dresses from this area. The dresses were so colorful and had beautiful, detailed designs. They were a wonderful sight to see!

Karamel felt so amazed! She understood that the old clothes and styles people wear in Türkiye tell us a lot about their history and how special their culture is. She went back home, thinking about her next exciting trip.
