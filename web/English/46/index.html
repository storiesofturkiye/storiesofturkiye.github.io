Karamel, the curious little cat, wanted to learn about the ancient myths and legends of Turkey. She decided to visit different regions to discover these fascinating stories and the mythical creatures within them. With excitement in her heart, she set off on a new adventure.

The Legend of the Phoenix – Hakkari Karamel’s first stop was Hakkari, where she heard the legend of the Phoenix, a mythical bird that is reborn from its ashes. She learned about the symbolism of rebirth and immortality.

The Story of the Simurgh - Mount Kaf Next, Karamel traveled to the mythical Mount Kaf in search of the Simurgh, a giant bird said to possess all the knowledge of the world. She listened to tales of the Simurgh’s wisdom and power.

9
The Tale of Shahmaran – Tarsus In Tarsus, Karamel discovered the tale of Shahmaran, a mythical creature that is half-woman, half-snake. She learned about Shahmaran’s wisdom, beauty, and the secret healing powers she possessed.

11
The Legend of Aspendos – Antalya Karamel traveled to Antalya, to hear the legend of Aspendos, a tale about a young architect who built an extraordinary theater to win the hand of a princess. She marveled at the grandeur of the ancient theater.

13
The Myth of the Anatolian Dragon – Erzurum In Erzurum, Karamel learned about the myth of the Anatolian Dragon, a fierce creature that guarded treasures in the mountains. She listened to stories of brave heroes who faced the dragon.
14
15
The Legend of the Lake Van Monster – Van Karamel’s next stop was Van, where she heard about the Lake Van Monster, a creature said to live in the depths of the lake. She explored the legends and stories surrounding this mysterious monster.

The Tale of the Golden Apple – Kazdağları Finally, Karamel visited Kazdağları (Mount Ida) to learn about the tale of the Golden Apple, a story of love and beauty that caused a great war among the gods. She discovered the mythical origins of this legendary story.

With her heart full of wonder and her mind enriched by the ancient myths, Karamel returned home. She realized that Türkiye’s mythology was filled with fascinating stories and magical creatures.
